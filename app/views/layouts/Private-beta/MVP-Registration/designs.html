High level research objectives

  1. Registration – to understand if waste receivers can register without support when they join the new digital waste tracking service, especially their understanding of registering and adding each business / organisation (concept of legal entity) separately.
  2. Data submission journeys – to evaluate waste receivers' understanding of being required to submit movement data separately per business / organisation, how their business / organisation might handle this, and what additional guidance might be required to support them to join the private beta service.
  3. Update information – to evaluate waste receivers' understanding of updating movement data, how they might handle this, and what additional guidance might be required.

High level research questions

  1. What implications does using a standardised version of Defra ID (without an organisations aggregation page) have, particularly those who have to register multiple legal entities?
  2. Do they understand (unprompted) that each legal entity needs to be registered, then add / register at least 2 of them?
  3. What additional workload does it place on them when they have to register multiple, and how might their company handle this? (centralised or distributed / delegated?)
  4. Do they understand (unprompted) that each legal entity needs to submit movement data separately, and how might they handle this? centralised or distributed / delegated?)
  5. Do they understand (unprompted) how to update waste movements, that a Waste Tracking ID will be needed, and that it will come from a previously uploaded submission being downloaded?
