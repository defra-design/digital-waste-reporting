Report receipt of waste

If you operate a permitted or licensed site which receives waste in the UK, you can use this service to:

  • register your business or organisation to report receipt of waste
  • access the alternative report receipt of waste method
  • sign your Receipt of waste declarations
  • pay your Report receipt of waste service charge

Before you start

Before you can use this service, you must:

  • Sign in or create a Government Gateway or OneLogin account for your business or organisation if you do not have one yet.
    • Do not use a Government Gateway account created to access your personal information.
  • Sign in or register for a business or organisation Defra account using this Government Gateway or OneLogin ID if you do not have one yet

Because you're registering on behalf of a business or organisation, you'll also need:

  • the main contact details for your business or organisation (for example, head office contact details)
  • the Companies House number if the business or organisation is a limited company

Setting up your admin account

The first account that you create for your business or organisation will automatically be an 'admin account'. This account can be used to create and approve additional users within the same business or organisation.

You can also add other users from your business or organisation to each account.

Read more about adding users to your account

If you need extra users for multiple legal entities, you can manage each legal entity's set of users individually inside your Defra account.

At least one user must be the person who is legally responsible for reporting the receipt of waste, usually the person who is currently responsible for managing your paper waste transfer notes or consignment notes.

The cost is Â£20 per year per individual legal entity, and gives the company the ability to report waste movements to Defra.

What is a legal entity?

Examples of legal entities include:

  • a sole trader
  • a company
  • a partnership
  • a limited liability partnership (LLP)
  • large businesses

In large businesses made up of multiple companies or other bodies, each individual limited company or body that has control over a permitted waste operation that receives controlled waste must pay the fee individually.

We use the term 'legal entity' interchangeably with 'business or organisation' in this service.

You'll need a debit or credit card to make your payment online.

When you have paid your annual service charge/s, we'll give you your API code, which will allow your software provider to set up the API.

What is an API?

Our new waste tracking service uses an API (Application Programming Interface). This is a set of rules that allow different software applications to talk to each other.

For example, you might already use your Google ID to sign into other apps and services. Or you might use PayPal to pay a bill. It's a convenient feature which uses APIs to authenticate users without asking them to create a new profile for every application.

Our API allows your waste reporting software to talk to our waste tracking software.

If you work with multiple software providers, there's an option to request multiple API codes.

Check with your software provider how to give them the API code, for example will they need it emailing or inputting directly into their app?

If you don't want to use the API code, you can use our alternative, spreadsheet-based reporting method. We'll add more details about this soon.

Get help with a technical issue

Contact the service support team if you need help with a technical issue, such as problems accessing the service.

Email: wastetracking@defra.gov.uk

Telephone: 0800 876 6458

The service support team will not be able to answer regulatory questions.

Get help with a regulatory issue

If you need help from a regulator to complete a receipt of waste record, you can contact your relevant regulatory team. They will not be able to answer technical questions about service problems.
